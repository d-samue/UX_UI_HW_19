Hi! I'm Danica Samuel

I’m a UX designer based in Minneapolis, MN and I love to sing, knit, crochet, and draw. When I create, I go in with the intention of creating authentic, accessible designs that I’ll be able to enjoy years from now.

Skills

User Experience Design

I feel that a big part of being a UX/UI designer is being able to advocate for your user. By utilizing data from user interviews, I interate and reiterate my designs to make sure I utilize our users' feedback in a way that makes them feel heard.

Web Development

With the help of my instructors and classmates, I've been able to develop and finetune my skills in Figma, Github, VS code, and Photopea.

Team Collaboration

I love working in teams because being able to work as a unit feels fulfilling. Creating a fun, creative, and collaborative environment is important to me because not only does it pave the way for a final design we're all proud of, but I get to know my teammates better in the process.

Data Synthesis & Analysis

I like contextualizing given data in easy to explain terms for clients and the design team, so we can all ideate and move forward from the same place of understanding.

My Work

App Design Project

Our group designed a CSA (community-supported agriculture) donation app. Through our research we found that a lot of people wanted to help their communities more, but found it difficult to find a donation option they could trust among the overwhelming amount of options. So in our design, we focused on transparency by educating users about CSAs and showing them what would be done with their donation/s.

Nonprofit Website Redesign

Our team remade the website for the American Choral Director Association for the Minnesota branch (desktop). We found that choral directors using the site had a hard time navigating it as it was disorganized and out of date. In the redesign, we each made our own iterations of the website and In each of our final designs we made sure to address the problems original site’s interface; mainly through improving accessibility; homogenizing graphics, limiting category sizes, etc.
